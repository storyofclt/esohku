Venture Capitalists in the Start-Up Ecosystem



Introduction

Invention and innovation drive the economy. Analysing and understanding the role played by venture capitalists is crucial for both - existing and future entrepreneurs. A venture capitalist is a private equity investor who provides capital support to companies that exhibit huge growth potential, in exchange for an equity stake. Venture capitalists usually fund start up ventures or small companies that don't have access to equity markets. They take the risk of investing in these companies as they stand to earn massive returns if these companies become successful. They usually target companies that are at the stage of commercialising their idea or about to enter the market.


A few features that venture capitalists tend to look for in a company include:

  • Strong, experienced management team
  • Large potential market
  • Unique product or service, with key competitive advantages
  • Scalability

Venture capital operates in a niche market, formed due to the structure and rules of capital markets. Entrepreneurs with ideas or a new technology often do not have any other institution to turn to for funding. Due to the risks associated with start-up ventures, banks charge a higher interest rate on loans, while entrepreneurs generally do not have the hard assets necessary to secure the debt. Additionally, equity markets too have their own restrictions in order to protect the public investor; generally, companies can access the public market only if they have millions in sales and assets and a reasonable profit history. To put this in perspective, an IPO on NASDAQ requires revenue of at least USD 11 million and assets of at least USD 80 million. Thus, venture capitalists fill this void between innovation and funding for that innovation.

It is very important for entrepreneurs to understand the venture capitalists’ motivations and whether these motivations align with their objectives, before securing any partnerships. Some of the most well-known venture capitalists include Jeremy Levine, Facebook’s first investor and founder of PayPal, Chris Sacca, an early investor in Twitter and Uber, and Jim Breyer, an early Facebook investor.


Role of Venture Capitalists in Hong Kong

In the past, most institutional investors focused on traditional investments such as the real estate and stock market, with only a small percentage of investors directing their investment towards startups. However, David Chang mentioned that many traditional investors have started to change their risk-averse attitudes and are more keen to include startups as part of their investment portfolios.

Venture capital firms now play a crucial role in building the startup ecosystem in Hong Kong. As startups are known to be high-risk and high-reward with more than 90% of startups fail to succeed, venture capital firms play an important role in identifying high-potential companies and investing in them. Venture Capitals are generally known to support startups when they are in their expansion stages, such as when they have started to generate a certain level of revenue.

A successful case: Lalamove

Founded in 2013, Lalamove, a logistics firm that provides on-demand delivery service, could not have been successful without the strong funding provided by venture capital firms. It is one of the few Hong Kong startups that has reached ‘unicorn’ status, which describes startups that have over $1 billion in valuation. As of December 2020, it has raised $461.5 million in funding and has operations in 24 cities across Asia, United States and Latin America.

Mindworks is a venture capital firm that has provided six consecutive rounds of funding to Lalamove. Other venture capital firms that recently led their $300 million D series funding include Sequoia Capital China and Hillhouse capital.


Conclusion

In conclusion, venture capital is an important tool to encourage entrepreneurship and it has helped build the start up ecosystem of Hong Kong. By emphasizing on the concepts of innovation and invention, entrepreneurs have been able to convert their ideas into viable projects. The financial assistance given out by venture capitalists fuels the potential talent of entrepreneurs and inspires them to turn around.
